What Is SGPA?

SGPA full form: Semester Grade Point Average. There are various ways to calculate academic performance in schools, colleges, and universities, one of which is the SGPA. Converting SGPA to a percentage is often important, especially at the end of a semester or academic year. To achieve a strong final score in your course, it’s important to maintain a good SGPA throughout. Doing so not only helps you secure better results but also gives you a clear picture of your progress and highlights the areas where you need improvement.

How to Calculate SGPA?

Now that you understand the concept of SGPA and its impact on your final score, let’s move forward. To calculate SGPA, follow these steps:

  1. Find the credit points for each subject.
  2. Multiply the credit points of each subject by the grade you earned in that subject.
  3. Add up all these results.
  4. Divide the total by the sum of all the credits.
  5. The final value you get will be your SGPA.

Formula

Here is the simple formula used to convert SGPA to percentage:

Percentage = (SGPA × 10) – 7.5

Example

How to calculate a student’s SGPA and convert it into a percentage for the four semesters:

  • First Semester: 5.0
  • Second Semester: 4.2
  • Third Semester: 5.4
  • Fourth Semester: 6.5

Solution:

SGPA = (5.0 + 4.2 + 5.4 + 6.5) / 4 = 21.1 / 4 = 5.275
Percentage = (5.275 × 10) – 7.5 = 52.75 – 7.5 = 45.25%
