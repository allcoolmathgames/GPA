Understanding Cumulative GPA

Your cumulative GPA is a weighted average of all grades received in all courses taken throughout your academic career. It provides a comprehensive overview of your overall academic performance by combining your previous academic achievements with your latest semester's results.

{# Added centered-section for consistency #}

Tips for Improving Your GPA

To improve your cumulative GPA, focus on performing exceptionally well in your current and upcoming courses, especially those with higher credit hours. Consider seeking academic support, forming study groups, and regularly attending office hours with your instructors. Prioritize your most challenging subjects and ensure you have a clear understanding of the material.

FAQ

How is the required GPA calculated?

It's calculated based on your current GPA, total credits, and the credits you plan to take in the next semester to reach your goal.

What if my goal GPA is unrealistic?

The calculator will show a very high or impossible required GPA. You may need to adjust your goal or study harder.

Can I adjust my goal GPA later?

Yes, you can always re-enter new values to calculate a different required GPA.
