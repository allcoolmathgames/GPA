How Do I Calculate High School GPA?

Calculating high school GPA is simple once you understand the steps. Most schools use a 4.0 scale, where an A is worth 4.0 points, a B is 3.0, a C is 2.0, a D is 1.0, and an F is 0. Follow these steps to calculate high school grade point average manually.

  • Write down your classes and the grades you received.
  • Convert each grade into points using the GPA scale.
  • For example, if you got an A in Math, that’s 4.0 points.
  • Multiply each grade point by the credit hours for that class.
  • If Math is worth 3 credits, then 4.0 × 3 = 12 grade points.
  • Do this for all your classes.
  • Add all the grade points together.
  • Add all the credit hours together.
  • Divide the total grade points by the total credit hours.

Manual calculations will take your time; simply use our GPA calculator high school to make calculations quick and easy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are credit hours, and how do I know them?

Credit hours represent the weight of a course. Most high school classes are 1 credit, but some (like labs or electives) might be 0.5 or more.

Why is calculating my High School GPA important?

Calculating your high school GPA is important because it shows your overall academic performance. Colleges, scholarship programs, and even some job applications often use grade point average to evaluate how well you’ve done in school.
